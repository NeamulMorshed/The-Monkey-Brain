## TL;DR
Bush's essay proposed the **memex**, a private desk-sized device for storing personal
records and linking them by **associative trails** rather than fixed hierarchical filing —
the first articulated vision of something like hypertext. He solved retrieval but not upkeep:
who builds and maintains the trails over time was left open.

## Key takeaways
- Associative, link-based retrieval over hierarchical filing — the core idea **wiki cross-links**
  in this vault descend from.
- A **private, curated** store, not a public one — closer to The Monkey Brain's per-project
  isolation than to the open web.
- The unsolved problem in 1945 — **who maintains the connections** — is the problem
  [[llm-wiki-pattern]] claims an LLM maintainer finally solves.
